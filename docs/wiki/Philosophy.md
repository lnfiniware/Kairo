# Philosophy

KairoDB is more than just a database tool; it is a philosophy of software development.

## 1. Human-First
Software should be built for humans. We prioritize readability and simplicity over complexity and "enterprise" features. If a human can't understand the schema at a glance, it's too complex.

## 2. Minimal
We believe in the power of "enough." We don't add features just because we can. We add features because they solve a real problem for real developers.

## 3. Honest
No marketing hype. No "AI-powered synergy." Just solid engineering that works. We are transparent about what KairoDB can and cannot do.

## 4. Local-First
Development should happen where the developer is—locally. KairoDB encourages a workflow where your data structure lives in your repository, not behind a SaaS wall.

## 5. Git-Friendly
Database structures should be version-controlled just like your code. By using plain-text `.kairo` files, we make it easy to track changes, review PRs, and maintain a history of your data evolution.
